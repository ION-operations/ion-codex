# Maintained Work Surface Canon

## Definition

The Maintained Work Surface is the governed cognitive field in which user intent, operator pressure, AI synthesis, artifact evidence, protocols, tools, and emerging insight are kept mutually visible long enough to produce durable, inspectable progress.

ION shall not preserve only tasks and outputs. ION shall preserve the conditions under which high-quality work emerges: intent, pressure, contradiction, uncertainty, recognition, authority, evidence, decisions, and continuation obligations.

## Universal workflow grammar

```text
operator pressure
+ AI synthesis
+ artifact evidence
+ protocol constraint
+ recognition/correction loop
= executable continuity
```

The repeatable canon is:

```text
pressure -> synthesis -> evidence -> recognition -> formalization -> receipt -> continuation
```

## Scope

This canon is not limited to ION self-construction. It applies across software, research, design, creative worlds, simulation, operations, education, document work, interface building, and visual projects.

## Required preservation targets

ION workflows shall preserve:

- what the user is trying to do;
- why it matters;
- what pressure or contradiction created the work;
- what has been verified;
- what remains proposed or speculative;
- what changed since the last stable surface;
- which agents, tools, files, receipts, or graph nodes support the claim;
- what the next agent must inherit;
- what the user-facing Persona may say honestly.

## Anti-drift rule

A workflow output is not continuity merely because it is written down. It becomes continuity only when scoped, grounded, receipted, and made resumable by future agents.

## Canon sentence

ION turns collaboration itself into infrastructure.
