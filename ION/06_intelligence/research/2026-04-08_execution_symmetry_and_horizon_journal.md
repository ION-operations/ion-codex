# Execution Symmetry and Horizon Journal

## Core correction

The project's real center is not manual versus automated workflow. The center is one canonical workflow whose carrier can shift.

## Implication

Every automation surface should be interpretable as one step that a capable executor could also carry manually when automation is unavailable.

## Second implication

The system should not only know the next step. It should maintain a horizon:
- precise immediate work,
- structured near work,
- looser far work that tightens over time.

## Why this matters

This keeps the kernel from behaving like a prompt-response box. It becomes an orchestration organism whose next window is progressively shaped by prior lawful work.
