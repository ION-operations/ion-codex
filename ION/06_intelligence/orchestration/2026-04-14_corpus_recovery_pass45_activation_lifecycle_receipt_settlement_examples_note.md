# Corpus recovery pass 45 — activation/lifecycle receipt and settlement worked examples

## What this pass adds
- a worked-example packet for activation/lifecycle receipt and settlement pressure
- a matrix covering accepted settlement, deferred settlement, escalated settlement, and cross-carrier final settlement
- a worked-examples note showing that receipts witness events while settlement classifies closure without rewriting enactment authority

## Why this matters
Carrier-crossing examples reduced the risk that carrier choice would quietly become authority.
This pass reduces the next adjacent risk: that receipts or settlement logic would quietly become a hidden second authority layer.

The project now has first-form examples showing that:
- activation receipts preserve enactment decision lineage,
- lifecycle witnesses preserve executor transition lineage,
- runtime/carrier receipts preserve what actually happened,
- and settlement classifies closure, deferment, escalation, or future re-entry without retroactively changing whether enactment was lawful.

## Resulting judgment
The activation/lifecycle candidate is now stronger under explicit history closure and future re-entry pressure.
It remains non-ratified and not thaw-ready.

The clearest remaining blocker is now install-path mapping into active architecture, followed by one bounded thaw-readiness reassessment after that mapping is reviewed.
