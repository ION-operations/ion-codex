# Lane A meta-template surface-design outlines

These outlines are **proposal-space only**.
They are candidate shapes for future current-line protocol drafting after review.

## 1. `META_TEMPLATE_CONSTITUTION_PROTOCOL.md`

### Proposed purpose
State why template development exists as a standing constitutional function, what class of work it governs, and how it relates to workflow law, kernel law, template binding, and later mutation carriers.

### Proposed sections
1. Purpose
2. Constitutional law
3. Canonical objects
4. Template-development jurisdiction
5. Standing constitutional obligations
6. Relation to workflow and template binding
7. Relation to state-machine / compiler seriousness
8. Relation to template-surface evolution and change carriers
9. Ratification prerequisites
10. Non-goals

### Candidate constitutional obligations
- preserve explicit gap analysis before new template invention,
- preserve relationship mapping across neighboring surfaces,
- preserve failure-mode anticipation rather than post-hoc cleanup,
- preserve automation-hook and registration seriousness,
- preserve anti-false-canon caution during restoration.

### Candidate core law
- Template development is a standing constitutional function, not an exceptional repair trick.
- Constitutional template law must remain upstream of mutation carriers and downstream of workflow truth.
- Meta-template law must be serious enough to constrain restoration, not merely decorate it.
- Constitutional restoration must not discard the current branch’s provenance and boundary discipline.

## 2. `TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT.md`

### Proposed purpose
State how a new template is lawfully developed from need discovery through evidence, registration, and downstream propagation, while remaining distinct from later mutation/change packets.

### Proposed sections
1. Purpose
2. Development law
3. Canonical objects
4. Development intent classes
5. Gap and relationship analysis
6. Failure-mode and automation-hook design
7. Registration and propagation duties
8. Evidence and readiness requirements
9. Relation to template-surface evolution/change
10. Non-goals

### Candidate development intent classes
- `NEW_SURFACE_REQUIRED`
- `GAP_REPAIR_REQUIRED`
- `RELATION_REPAIR_REQUIRED`
- `AUTOMATION_HOOK_REQUIRED`
- `REGISTRATION_ALIGNMENT_REQUIRED`

### Candidate core law
- Template development must begin from a real gap, relation, failure, or propagation need.
- Every new template surface must carry explicit registration obligations.
- Development packets must remain distinct from mutation packets even when they lead to future change.
- A template is not complete until its neighboring surfaces and downstream registration duties are accounted for.

## Integration note

The two candidate protocol surfaces should be drafted only as a bundle.
Drafting one without the other would risk recreating the same bridge-overload ambiguity Lane A already separated.
