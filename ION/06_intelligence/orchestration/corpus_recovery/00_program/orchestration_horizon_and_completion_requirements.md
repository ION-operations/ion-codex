# Orchestration horizon and completion requirements

## Purpose

In ION, a workflow should never begin as an unknown trajectory.

This does **not** mean every operation must begin with a rigid fixed-phase master plan.
It **does** mean every operation must begin with an explicit orchestration understanding of:
- where it is starting,
- what near / mid / far horizons currently look like,
- what counts as complete enough for the current pass,
- what would force reassessment,
- and what the current process position is.

## Minimum orchestration understanding

Before work begins, the work-start record should state all of the following.

### 1. Current process position
What phase or position is the work currently in?
Examples:
- atlas consultation
- compare-centers stage
- recovery stage
- bridge-repair stage
- bounded implementation stage
- verification/receipt stage

### 2. Near horizon
What is the next immediate objective?
What would count as complete enough for this pass?

### 3. Mid horizon
What larger subproblem is this pass serving?
What should become clearer, cleaner, or more controlled if the near horizon succeeds?

### 4. Far horizon
What longer-running organism-level aim is this contributing to?
Examples:
- Milestone 1 atlas stabilization
- activation-center recovery
- template-center restoration
- reintegration planning

### 5. Completion condition
What does “done” mean **for this pass**?
Not forever. Not for the whole project.
For this pass.

### 6. Non-completion boundary
What is explicitly *not* being completed in this pass?
This is required to stop false momentum and false closure.

### 7. Reassessment triggers
What would force the orchestration to stop, change posture, or reopen center selection?
Examples:
- stronger conflicting evidence appears
- the primary center proves weaker than expected
- executable proof fails
- missing evidence blocks lawful continuation

## Dynamic but coherent rule

The orchestration may evolve.
Its horizons may change.
Its sequence may branch or compress.

But it should never be absent.
At every meaningful work start, the project should know:
- what kind of work this is,
- where it is in the process,
- what near/mid/far aims currently are,
- and what would count as complete enough right now.

## Why this matters

A project can drift even with good question classes, center selection, posture selection, and output classes if it still begins work with no explicit sense of:
- current process position,
- completion target,
- or horizon.

This requirement exists to stop that form of drift.
