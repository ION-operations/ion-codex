# Milestone 1 completion judgment

## Current judgment
**Milestone 1 is achieved as a provisional stable atlas base.**

This does **not** mean the project is reconciled, finished, or final canon.
It means the recovery program is now strong enough to function as the first trustworthy atlas base for future work.

## Why the judgment is now positive
The project now has all of the following in-project and evidence-backed:
- archive register and family index
- prior-audit register and failure register
- fractured-core profiles
- values/soul recovery surfaces
- conflict and missing-evidence registers
- center-status and child-root status judgments
- executable receipts for the major missing historical centers:
  - `ION-BUILD` runtime/API/session line
  - `operation-victus` manager/orchestrator line
  - `Project-Gemini` continuation witness
  - SOS-family compact executable witnesses
  - compact precursor pair
  - `IONv2`
  - `AETHER-OS-V4`

## Why the judgment remains provisional
The atlas base is still provisional because:
- not every supporting line has equal executable depth
- the historical activation center is evidenced but not reintegrated into the current branch
- the historical meta-template center is judged stronger than the current bridge-era repair, but is not yet restored into a new constitutional center
- some tensions are intentionally preserved instead of flattened:
  - compactness versus explicitness
  - precursor pair versus current branch
  - `IONv2` versus current branch
  - historical template-development center versus current bridge repair

## What this base is good for
This atlas base is now strong enough to:
- orient a fresh human or AI worker
- prevent obvious false replacement of already-built systems
- distinguish real centers from wrappers, siblings, and peripheral witnesses
- guide targeted recovery and reintegration work

## What it must not be used for
It must not be treated as:
- final consensus canon
- proof that all contradictions are resolved
- license to erase the conflict register
- a substitute for later reintegration work
