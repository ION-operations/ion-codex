# Activation/Lifecycle Interface — Review Note

## Minimal formulation

The activation/lifecycle seam is the lawful handoff between:
- an **activation decision** that authorizes enactment,
- and the first **executor lifecycle transitions** that bind and enter a specific executor.

Activation owns the question:
**May this bounded enactment cross into executable work?**

Lifecycle owns the question:
**Given lawful permission, how does the chosen executor move through claim, readiness, entry, suspension, return, release, failure, and retirement?**

## Shared contract

The seam should carry, at minimum:

- bounded work identity,
- enactment permission class,
- scope and side-effect boundary,
- eligible carrier/executor posture,
- required prerequisites,
- continuity reference if present,
- and an activation receipt identity that lifecycle can cite.

## Non-negotiable boundary rules

1. Lifecycle may not silently reinterpret `ALLOW` into a new authority judgment.
2. Lifecycle may block `CLAIM`, `READY`, or `ENTER` when prerequisites fail, but that block must cite readiness or integrity failure rather than re-deciding activation authority.
3. Resume or re-entry must either:
   - cite preserved authority plus validated continuity,
   - or request fresh activation.
4. Carrier-shell differences do not create different seams.

## Short judgment

The activation/lifecycle pair is now coherent enough to review as a set because the handoff between them can be stated in narrow contract form rather than left to implication.
