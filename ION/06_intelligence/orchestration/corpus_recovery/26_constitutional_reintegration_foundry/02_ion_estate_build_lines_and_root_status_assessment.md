# ION Estate Build Lines and Root Status Assessment

## Why this exists

The production estate contains too many roots to let the word "current" carry
enough meaning by itself. Some roots are stale for startup but still crucial as
historical executable centers. Some are compact but runnable witnesses. Some are
supporting atlas lines. Some are wrappers. Some are active candidate canon.

This assessment therefore distinguishes:

- what kind of center each root is,
- what it preserves best,
- what it should not be mistaken for,
- and what "stale" actually means in this estate.

The most important clarification is that stale does not mean valueless.
In this estate, many stale-for-startup roots are indispensable-for-reintegration
roots.

## First distinction: stale for what?

The estate benefits from a four-part stale vocabulary.

A surface can be stale for startup, meaning a fresh executor should not treat it
as the first active center. A surface can be stale for direct implementation,
meaning it should not be edited as if it were still the primary runtime line. A
surface can still be current for witness value, meaning its preservation is
essential to honest adjudication. And a surface can be current for extraction,
meaning it is the strongest candidate to receive further work even if the
workspace-level canon remains unsettled.

Many past confusions came from using "stale" as if it collapsed all four of
those judgments into one.

## Executive status map

At the coarsest level, the estate currently appears to contain these classes:

the stronger packaged current branch, the older top-level production `ION/`
line, several extracted branch variants, the `ION-BUILD` and Aether
constitutional/runtime line, the Victus/Gemini manager-orchestrator line, the
SOS sibling family, the compact witness line (`IONv2` plus precursor `ION`
variants), the atlas/provenance support line, and an adjacent AIM-ION / AIM-OS
universe that is relevant but not yet cleanly integrated into the recovery
atlas.

## 1. The current-branch family

The most important current family is no longer just the top-level production
`ION/` root. The stronger packaged branch under
`ION most recent/ion_current_canonical_runtime_fleet_temporal_2026-04-16/ION/`
is now the best current candidate center because it carries a denser and later
orchestration spine, explicit repo authority, current-generation ratification,
Steward current-phase orchestration truth, corpus recovery, controlled
reintegration, runtime/session review-entry material, fleet lifecycle work, and
temporal stack additions.

That packaged branch is not yet the sole workspace-level canon, because the
top-level `ION/` root still preserves MCP, API, docs/program, and preflight
surfaces that the packaged branch does not carry. But the packaged branch is the
most coherent current narrative center and the strongest candidate for future
promotion.

Within this family, `ION current/`, `ION_Working Branch/`, `ION_Working_Branch_M3/`,
and `ION_Working_Branch_M16/` should be treated primarily as extracted witness
variants in a current-branch chain, not as independent organism centers.

Their main value is lineage, historical packet continuity, and diffable witness
state. Their main danger is that a fresh worker can mistake path names for
competing current roots.

## 2. The top-level production `ION/` root

The top-level `ION/` root is still runnable and still operationally meaningful,
but it is no longer enough by itself to represent the current best picture of
the organism.

Its real importance now is threefold. First, it preserves a practical runnable
entry surface with MCP/API/docs/program orientation. Second, it preserves an
older but still useful production-shaped line that helps show what was kept
small and operator-facing before the newer packaged branch thickened again.
Third, it is the other half of the present root split, which means it must be
adjudicated rather than quietly ignored.

So this root is stale as sole canon, but not stale as a live authority problem.

## 3. The precursor compact witness pair

The recovery atlas already classifies production `ION` and `ION (codex branch)`
as compact extracted-core witnesses. That judgment remains strong.

What these lines preserve is not broad organism completeness. What they preserve
is compactness, runnable evidence, and smaller-surface discipline. They are
valuable because they show what later lines made heavier, more explicit, or more
administratively broad.

Their main misread risk is that compactness can be romanticized into total
superiority, or their runnable status can tempt a worker to declare them the
whole organism. Neither is honest. They are compact witnesses, not the total
answer.

## 4. `IONv2`

`IONv2` remains one of the most important compact executable witnesses in the
estate. It is easy to underweight because it is smaller than the current branch
and less richly narrated than the newer orchestration surfaces. That is exactly
why it matters.

It preserves compact implementation discipline around graph/context/package
behavior and governed-write lineage. It is a strong counterexample against the
idea that every important truth in the estate requires the heaviest possible
surface area.

It is stale as the singular future branch, but current as a compactness and
discipline witness.

## 5. `ION-BUILD`

`ION-BUILD` is still the strongest historical executable runtime/API/session
center and also one of the strongest preserved Aether constitutional centers.

That statement matters because it means `ION-BUILD` is not just an older build
you read for color. It is a root whose missing capabilities define the present
reintegration gap: runtime sessions, queueing, API entry, Aether constitution,
Aether atlas, and template-development seriousness.

It is stale as immediate startup center for the current branch, but current as a
primary reintegration source. Any honest future ION that claims runtime/session
completeness while never passing through `ION-BUILD`-level adjudication is
probably faking wholeness.

## 6. `operation-victus` and `Project-Gemini`

These two roots preserve the strongest executable manager/orchestrator/swarm
center in the estate. The atlas is correct to treat them as a shared line with
`Project-Gemini` as a strong continuation witness rather than a completely
independent origin.

Their importance is not that they prove the current branch should become a
swarm-first architecture immediately. Their importance is that they preserve the
most explicit activation/orchestrator logic and multi-agent cycle evidence that
the current branch still lacks.

They are stale as direct current canon, but current as the strongest preserved
activation/manager truth. That means they belong in reintegration planning, not
in nostalgic side-reading.

## 7. The SOS family

`SOS`, `SOS-OPUS`, and `SOS-Gemini` are mixed sibling families. The recovery
atlas is right not to reduce them to pure doctrine residue.

These roots are structurally rich and partly executable, but they are not the
best primary center for the total organism now. Their lasting value is in
sovereign doctrine, protocol structures, chassis and governance language, and
template-development lineage. In many cases they preserve the older legal and
coordination imagination that later lines partially translated, partially
narrowed, and partially lost.

They are stale as startup canon, but not stale as sibling constitutional and
governance evidence.

## 8. `AETHER-OS-V4`

`AETHER-OS-V4` is neither the current branch nor merely decorative residue. It
is a sibling executable witness for an Aether-facing OS/runtime shell direction.

Its main value is showing the project’s OS-facing ambition in a more explicit
runtime/application shell form. It should not outrank `ION-BUILD` or the current
branch, but it should also not be collapsed into "old doctrine."

So it is a secondary supporting witness: stale for startup, current for shell
ambition and runtime packaging lineage.

## 9. `00_CONSOLIDATED_ATLAS`, `ATLAS`, and `ProjectOpus`

These are archaeology and support centers, not primary executable organism
centers. But they are much more than loose notes.

`00_CONSOLIDATED_ATLAS` is the strongest prior authority/lineage ledger line and
still acts as the embryo of a true adjudication substrate. `ATLAS` is an
external systems-comparative library that gives the project field vocabulary and
cross-lineage grounding. `ProjectOpus` preserves anti-chaos and
contradiction-preserving consolidation method.

These roots are stale for direct implementation authority, but current for
meta-governance and recovery method.

## 10. `composeraudit`, `geminiaudit`, and `conjugate-basis-hidden-field`

These should remain explicitly non-primary. The atlas is correct here.

The two audit wrapper roots are valuable wrapper witnesses around atlas and
consolidation work, but they should never silently inflate into organism
centers. `conjugate-basis-hidden-field` is a peripheral executable witness and
possibly a useful conceptual control line, but it should not be promoted into a
foundational ontology center without a separate serious adjudication.

These roots are current as witness matter and stale as center candidates.

## 11. `AIM-ION` and the AIM-OS universe

`AIM-ION` sits in a different category from the roots above. It is not simply
one more ION historical branch. It is a curated orphan copy of a much broader
adjacent organism, AIM-OS, with its own truth packs, operational spines,
archives, and grand architectural claims.

That makes it both relevant and dangerous. It is relevant because it preserves
powerful systems around memory, provenance, model routing, MCP collaboration,
context federation, operational gate discipline, and operator surfaces. It is
dangerous because its own archive mass, identity sprawl, subsystem count, and
self-description can encourage over-importing or category mistakes.

So `AIM-ION` should currently be treated as an adjacent sibling universe and
pattern library, not yet as an already-integrated ION line.

## 12. Unclear or low-priority supporting roots

Roots such as `ClaudePortal` may still hold important material, but they are not
yet strongly classified in the current recovery atlas. They should be treated as
unadjudicated support/peripheral lines until explicit evidence raises or lowers
their status.

That is an important discipline point: unclassified does not mean irrelevant,
but it also does not mean implicitly canonical.

## Implications

The estate does not suffer from a lack of material. It suffers from a lack of
stable adjudicated precedence across material classes.

The most important present implication is therefore not "find the newest root"
or "find the root that runs." The more important question is "which center is
strongest for which missing truth, and what current branch should receive that
truth under controlled reintegration?"

This is why the next serious work cannot just be feature building. It has to be
root adjudication, line-by-line preservation profiling, and eventually
canonicalization decisions with retained witness value.

## Recommended next moves

The estate should now explicitly distinguish the stronger packaged current line
from the top-level `ION/` root, rather than letting both remain ambient current
claims.

It should also explicitly classify `AIM-ION` as a sibling/pattern universe in
the reintegration atlas until a deeper decision is made.

And future build-line work should prefer explicit profile and adjudication
surfaces over free-form comparative prose, which is exactly why the current
phase now has `SYSTEM_LINEAGE_PROFILE` and `CANONICALIZATION_DECISION` support
templates.
