## Purpose
Demonstrate that the activation/lifecycle candidate survives explicit receipt lineage and settlement classification without letting downstream history rewrite upstream lawfulness.

This packet does **not** thaw the candidate.
This packet does **not** install active law.
This packet does **not** replace install-path mapping.

## Why this review is necessary
Carrier-crossing examples showed that enactment meaning stays stable across manual, daemon, API, and continuation variation.
What still remained was proof-by-example that the same candidate also survives:
- activation receipts,
- lifecycle transition witnesses,
- runtime/carrier receipts,
- settlement outcomes,
- and lawful future re-entry

without collapsing those into one hidden authority center.

Without receipt/settlement examples, the repo can still drift into three old mistakes:
- treating receipts as if they grant or revoke authority,
- treating settlement as if it can retroactively rewrite whether enactment was lawful,
- or treating future re-entry as if it can appear without an explicit settled path.

## Worked-example classes

### 1. Successful enactment with accepted settlement
A bounded task is lawfully activated, executed, receipted, and closed with explicit accepted settlement.

Review question:
Can the repo show that settlement closes the history by citing receipt lineage rather than by restating activation/lifecycle from scratch?

### 2. Suspended work with deferred settlement and future re-entry
A task crosses lawfully into enactment, returns early with preserved continuity, and settlement defers closure pending later re-entry.

Review question:
Can the repo show that deferred settlement preserves lawful history without pretending the work was never activated or already fully closed?

### 3. Failed enactment with escalated settlement
A task enters enactment lawfully, but runtime or side-effect failure occurs after entry.

Review question:
Can the repo show that settlement may escalate review or classify failure without retroactively converting lawful activation into unlawful activation?

### 4. Cross-carrier resumed completion with final settlement
A previously activated task suspends in one carrier, resumes in another under preserved or refreshed authority as required, and later settles finally.

Review question:
Can the repo show receipt lineage across carrier change and make settlement the closer of the chain rather than the source of fresh authority?
