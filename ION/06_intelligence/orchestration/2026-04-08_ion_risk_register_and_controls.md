# ION Risk Register and Controls

## R1 — Workflow/packet ambiguity

### Risk
The next executor still has to infer too much from prose or scattered files.

### Control
- standardize packet taxonomy
- keep canonical examples current
- add packet validation where useful

## R2 — Witness overshadowing core workflow

### Risk
Support/report surfaces visually compete with the actual organism.

### Control
- keep support layers explicitly subordinate
- avoid default growth in witness families
- keep root read order centered on workflow and execution

## R3 — Manual/automation divergence

### Risk
Automated steps become unavailable or incomprehensible when runtime is off.

### Control
- define manual fallback for each critical automated step
- run equivalence rehearsals
- treat carrier symmetry as a release gate

## R4 — Hidden step jumps

### Risk
A daemon/service path compresses several unstated transitions and harms takeover.

### Control
- insist on one bounded step
- emit receipts/packets for every real transition
- prefer lawful explicitness over convenience

## R5 — Handoff insufficiency

### Risk
Another executor cannot continue from lawful artifacts alone.

### Control
- takeover bundle standardization
- limited-read continuation tests
- stronger router/session discipline

## R6 — Swarm before settlement law

### Risk
Parallel work is introduced before merge, review, and budget controls exist.

### Control
- block swarm claims until merge law exists
- gate fan-out on concurrency budgets and explicit review paths

## R7 — Horizon fiction

### Risk
Immediate/near/far becomes ceremonial language instead of live state.

### Control
- keep horizon record families active
- test tightening behavior explicitly
- make horizon part of scenario suites

## R8 — External authority leakage

### Risk
External/API carriers are treated as direct truth.

### Control
- keep external returns proposal-only
- require identical landing gates
- keep bridge receipts and proposed deltas explicit

## R9 — Recovery overclaim

### Risk
Replay/resume semantics become hidden autonomy.

### Control
- require explicit replay requests unless policy says otherwise
- record stale/resumable classifications clearly
- keep replay re-entering the same live policy/control path

## R10 — Lead-only comprehension

### Risk
The project becomes operable only by someone who already “gets it.”

### Control
- maintain top-level index/read order
- keep walkthroughs current
- prioritize operator entry surface and scenario documentation

## R11 — Protocol proliferation without synthesis

### Risk
New protocol files accumulate faster than integration clarity.

### Control
- require every new protocol to map to the canonical loop
- prefer updating existing authoritative docs over adding near-duplicate ones
- maintain the orchestration index as the synthesis surface

## R12 — Test confidence illusion

### Risk
Broad unit coverage substitutes for end-to-end truth.

### Control
- grow the rehearsal/scenario suite
- treat scenario failure as more serious than isolated unit success
- keep completion claims tied to scenario categories

## R13 — Scheduler remains implicit

### Risk
Executor choice, deferral, and horizon tightening stay scattered across helpers and operator intuition.

### Control
- define an explicit scheduler protocol
- create scheduler state / commitment gradient semantics
- add scheduling receipts and policy surfaces
- tie scheduler claims to rehearsal and acceptance evidence

## R14 — Premature schedule freezing

### Risk
The system overcommits far-horizon work and loses lawful flexibility.

### Control
- distinguish immediate, near, and far rigidity
- keep commitment gradient explicit
- require justification for tightening and rebinding
- preserve refusal paths for non-ready candidates

## R15 — Branch-aware work collapses into a fake linear queue

### Risk
Conditional continuations and merge obligations are flattened into a misleading single-list plan.

### Control
- model branch-sensitive scheduling explicitly
- keep settlement/merge law ahead of swarm widening
- preserve branch-local horizon semantics in later M-phase work
