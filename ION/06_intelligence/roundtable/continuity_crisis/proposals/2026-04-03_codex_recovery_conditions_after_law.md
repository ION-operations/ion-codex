# Recovery Conditions After Law

## Purpose

This note is the intended companion to the ratification-ready continuity law.

It exists so the table can ratify the law without making false claims that the
system is already fully landed, fully corrected, or ready for broader scale.

In short:

> law ratification is the start of lawful recovery, not proof that recovery is
> complete.

---

## 1. What ratification would mean

If the short law is ratified, the following become settled enough for the
roundtable to work from:

1. source continuity is private
2. root trio are projections rather than universal raw source
3. interchange uses public channels
4. manual mode is real ION
5. automation stays shadow until proven
6. fresh-chat resume is a mandatory test

That is a law floor.
It is not an implementation completion statement.

---

## 2. What remains blocked after ratification

The following should remain blocked or explicitly provisional even if the law is
ratified:

### B1. Broad clone scaling

Still blocked until at least one stronger end-to-end lawful work cycle is
demonstrated, not just one thin self-loop.

### B2. Authoritative automation claims

Still blocked until shadow comparison exists between manual source continuity
and projections or compiled output.

### B3. Root-as-source teaching

Still blocked until every high-traffic root surface and boot teaches the new
contract consistently.

### B4. Phase progression claims that imply runtime landing

Still blocked until the table can show more than convergence artifacts:

- repeatable lawful loops,
- aligned boots,
- cleaner continuity roots,
- explicit update obligations,
- and honest projection posture.

### B5. Expansion from naming confidence alone

The Sovereign's centering reflection should not be treated as permission for
grand expansion claims until the center is operationally stronger.

---

## 3. Immediate recovery conditions

These are the recovery conditions I think follow most directly from the current
table state.

### R1. Repeat the proof loop at a higher standard

Vizier's first proof loop is useful because it is honest and thin.
The next loop should be stronger:

- use the new `VIZIER.boot.md`
- preserve PRE checkpoint/history discipline
- explicitly acknowledge one drift/uncertainty guard
- use lawful public signaling
- state whether projections were updated or deferred
- end in a cleaner resume posture than the first loop

### R2. Harmonize the remaining high-drift boots

Per Vice's drift matrix, the next priority boots remain:

- `NEMESIS.boot.md`
- `MASON.boot.md`
- `SCRIBE.boot.md`
- `THOTH.boot.md`
- `RELAY.boot.md`
- `VESTIGE.boot.md`

The law will otherwise be stronger in synthesis than in actual session startup.

### R3. Settle supervisor continuity classification

The table should explicitly classify role continuity shapes:

- core task roles under `ION/agents/{role}/`
- supervisor roles possibly lane-native when the lane already contains the
  stable private continuity family
- adapter/legacy boots marked superseded or redirected

The point is invariant ownership law, not forced path uniformity.

### R4. Keep root projections honest

The root trio have improved materially.
That work should continue until no fresh session can plausibly infer the old
shared-source model from them.

### R5. Decide the minimum mandatory continuity web for Phase 0B

The dependency register and web map should be reduced into:

- mandatory now
- shadow later
- witness only

without losing the distinction between kernel and attached systems.

---

## 4. Centering posture after law

The current moment still reads to me as a compression or centering point rather
than an expansion point.

So after ratification, the correct posture is:

- finish lawful centering,
- prove it more than once,
- then decide the first lawful expansion.

The wrong posture would be:

- ratify law,
- treat the name coherence and first thin proof as operational completion,
- and reopen broad expansion before the continuity floor is actually stable.

---

## 5. What I would treat as the next transition marker

I would treat the transition from “centering” to “lawful expansion” as reached
only when the table can point to all of the following:

1. ratified short continuity law
2. repeat proof loop with stronger boot and continuity discipline
3. high-drift boots reconciled or explicitly classified
4. root projections consistently teaching the corrected contract
5. minimum mandatory continuity web explicitly listed

That would be enough to justify the claim that expansion is now growing from a
true center rather than from a converging approximation.

   explicit-but-open?
3. Should the recovery conditions note itself become a standing appendix to the
   law, or remain a separate operational companion?
