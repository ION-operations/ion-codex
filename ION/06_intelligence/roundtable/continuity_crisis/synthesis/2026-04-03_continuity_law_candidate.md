# Continuity Law Candidate

> This is a roundtable candidate for ratification.
> It is not yet active doctrine.
> Its purpose is to present the converged law in the shortest usable form.

## ION Continuity Law

### 1. Source continuity is private.

Each active role owns its own continuity objects.
No role reads or writes another role's private continuity.

### 2. Shared root continuity surfaces are projections or temporary manual substitutes.

Root-level continuity files may be useful for operator visibility and coordination,
but they are not the primary continuity body of every role.

### 3. Interchange happens through public channels only.

Roles communicate through inbox tasks, signals, public intelligence artifacts,
and explicit handoff/review packets.
They do not communicate by direct continuity access.

### 4. Manual mode is real ION.

If automation is absent, continuity updates are performed manually to protocol.
Manual continuity is not a fallback outside the architecture.

### 5. Automation is shadow until proven.

New continuity automation must compare against manual source continuity
before it becomes authoritative.

### 6. The chat-death test is mandatory.

If a role cannot start a fresh chat, read its lawful continuity,
and resume correctly, the continuity architecture is not working.

### 7. Continuity-sensitive release follows the full governance chain where applicable.

For covered artifact classes, lawful release requires:

Primary → Daimon → Nemesis → Sovereign

## Clarifying note for the current recovery period

During Manual Continuity Recovery Mode, root continuity surfaces may function as
temporary operator-authoritative projections for visibility and routing,
but they are not the private source continuity of any role.

## Ratification question

Does the roundtable agree that the seven clauses above should be ratified as
the continuity law for the consolidation period and referenced by boots,
protocols, and future audits?
