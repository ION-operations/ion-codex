# Meta-Template Constitution Protocol

## 1. Purpose

ION treats **template development** as a **standing constitutional function**: discovering real governance gaps, designing durable templates, evidencing them, registering them, and propagating obligations.

This protocol exists so meta-template law cannot be silently replaced by **mutation carriers** alone.

## 2. Constitutional law (current-line)

1. **Upstream of mutation carriers:** `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_EVOLUTION_PROTOCOL.md` and `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_CHANGE.md` govern *how existing or emerging surfaces move*. They do **not** satisfy the full constitutional role of *why template invention exists* or *what class of work template development governs*.

2. **Downstream of workflow truth:** `CANONICAL_WORKFLOW.md` and `AGENT_CONTRACT.md` remain supreme for executor behavior; template development must not invent parallel workflow.

3. **Anti-false-canon:** Restoration, recovery, and bridge-era work must not accidentally treat convenience templates as ratified final canon. Labels like `NOT_FINAL_CANON` remain operative until an explicit ratification packet says otherwise.

## 3. Canonical objects

- **Gap record:** documented missing template / missing obligation.
- **Relationship map:** neighboring templates, protocols, registries, boots.
- **Failure-mode register:** anticipated drift modes.
- **Automation-hook plan:** machine-readable obligations where applicable.
- **Registration receipt:** explicit placement in `ION/07_templates/` and registry/boot updates as required by the governing task.

## 4. Template-development jurisdiction

Template development governs **new or materially new template surfaces** and **template-class obligations** when:

- a repeated action lacks a governed template, or
- an existing template cannot absorb the obligation without overload, or
- a constitutional split (example: mutation carrier vs standing development law) reappears.

## 5. Standing constitutional obligations

Minimum obligations (aligned to Lane A outline candidates):

- Preserve **gap analysis** before invention.
- Preserve **relationship mapping** across neighboring surfaces.
- Preserve **failure-mode anticipation** rather than post-hoc cleanup only.
- Preserve **registration / propagation seriousness** (where the template lives, who must read it, what boots/registries update).
- Preserve **anti-false-canon caution** during restoration.

## 6. Relation to workflow and template binding

- Workflow doctrine selects *how work proceeds*.
- Template binding law selects *how roles refine shared templates*.
- Meta-template constitution selects *when and how new template law may be born*.

## 8. Relation to template-surface evolution and change carriers

When a new template is introduced or an existing template materially changes:

1. Follow `TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT.md` for **development discipline**.
2. Follow `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_EVOLUTION_PROTOCOL.md` and use `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_CHANGE.md` for **lawful mutation provenance**.

## 9. Ratification prerequisites

Promotion from `NOT_FINAL_CANON` / bridge posture to stricter canon requires **its own bounded ratification packet** (not this file alone).

## 10. Non-goals

- Does not widen canonical packet validator families by itself.
- Does not authorize silent bulk imports from sibling estates without review packets.
- Does not merge meta-template law back into the mutation carrier.
