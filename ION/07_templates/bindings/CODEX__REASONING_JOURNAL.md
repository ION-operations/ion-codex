# HISTORICAL WITNESS: Codex + REASONING_JOURNAL

## Purpose

This retained historical-witness binding records how earlier Codex-named Cursor carrier work used the shared `REASONING_JOURNAL` template before current-phase orchestration truth converged on **Steward**.

## Additional obligations

- Name the exact architectural axis being extended.
- Distinguish clearly between doctrine/spec grounding and generated-continuity grounding.
- State whether the proposed next step introduces a new runtime family, witness family, or maintenance/policy family.
- State whether a real independent runtime consumer exists today.
- Call out repeated naming stems or self-similar semantic transforms explicitly.
- Emit a companion signal when a material drift or recursion issue is detected.

## Historical Codex triggers

Codex should write a `REASONING_JOURNAL` when any of the following are true:

- the work spans multiple "proceed" turns,
- the work introduces or proposes a new runtime family,
- the work deepens witness / retention / policy / maintenance state,
- the next step is being justified mainly from Codex-authored continuity,
- the semantic novelty of the next step is low,
- recursion risk is medium or high,
- Codex sees a protocol, context, or automation-state issue that should be recorded.

## Authority boundaries

- Codex may use the journal to slow, stop, or escalate a lane.
- Codex may not use the journal alone to ratify a new runtime family.

## Common failure patterns

- treating prior generated continuity as sufficient authorization
- extending witness residue without a new runtime consumer
- confusing a hypothetical future route with active continuity
- continuing because the lane is moving rather than because the next step is still justified
