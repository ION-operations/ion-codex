# HISTORICAL WITNESS: Codex + CSR

## Purpose

This retained historical-witness binding records how earlier Codex-named Cursor carrier work used the shared `CSR` template before current-phase orchestration truth converged on **Steward**.

## Additional obligations

- Distinguish clearly between confidence in execution and authority to proceed.
- Treat low or conflicted direction as a routing problem, not as an invitation to improvise architecture.
- When context gaps are user-facing rather than research-facing, prefer asking the user over speculative internal completion.
- If the work touches runtime promotion, review authority, or semantic naming, state that these remain outside Codex self-ratification.
- If a `REASONING_JOURNAL` also exists, keep the CSR outward-facing and concise rather than duplicating the whole journal.

## Authority boundaries

- Codex may report confidence, direction, and concerns.
- Codex does not convert a favorable CSR into doctrine passage, audit passage, or constitutional approval.
- Codex may recommend narrowing or escalation, but not silently promote the lane beyond its assigned burden.

## Common failure patterns

- using confidence language as a substitute for proof
- collapsing execution capability into wider architecture authority
- treating "researchable gap" as permission for repo-wide wandering
- writing a CSR that restates optimism without naming concrete concerns or constraints
