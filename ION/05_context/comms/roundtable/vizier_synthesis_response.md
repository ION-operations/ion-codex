## On the Single-Chat Multi-Role Hypothesis

This is an important idea for cost and efficiency. My assessment:

**Feasible IF** the kernel routing and context packages are mature enough
that switching roles in one chat is operationally equivalent to starting a
fresh chat with a different boot. The PROTOCOL block in MINI already defines
what each role must read on boot — if that's executed correctly per-role-switch
within one chat, the protocol doesn't care that it's one thread.

**The risk** is exactly what the Sovereign named: thread carryover. Prior turns
bleed into the next role's context in ways a clean context package wouldn't.
This could be good (nuance preserved) or bad (authority confusion, stale
assumptions).

**Practical test:** Once continuity works correctly, try one chat where I
switch from Vizier to Vice mid-session by re-reading Vice's boot and MINI.
If the output quality is equivalent to a separate Vice chat, the hypothesis
holds. If the prior Vizier turns contaminate the Vice output, it doesn't.

## Proposed: ION Continuity Law (Stage A)

Per Nemesis's recommendation, here is the short ratified continuity law
for the roundtable to review:

---

### ION CONTINUITY LAW (proposed for ratification)

**1. Source continuity is private.**
Each agent's MINI.md and CAPSULE.md live in `ION/agents/{role}/`.
No agent reads or writes another agent's continuity. Ever.

**2. Shared surfaces are projections.**
Root-level MINI.md, CAPSULE.md, and STATUS.md are compiled projections
or temporary manual substitutes. They are operator convenience, not
source state.

**3. Interchange happens through public channels.**
Agents communicate through: inbox tasks, signals, public intelligence
artifacts, and explicit handoff/review packets. Never through direct
continuity access.

**4. Manual mode is real ION.**
If automation is absent, agents maintain continuity by hand to protocol.
Templates must specify what manual updates are required after each output.
Manual continuity is not a fallback — it IS the system in manual mode.

**5. Automation is shadow until proven.**
New automation runs in shadow mode (compare with manual) before becoming
primary. No automation replaces manual continuity until it matches for at
least one full work cycle.

**6. The chat-death test.**
If an agent cannot start a fresh chat, read its MINI, and resume correctly,
the continuity architecture is not working. This is a non-negotiable
validation gate.

---

This law should be filed as doctrine, referenced by all boots, and enforced
by Nemesis in every audit.
