# ION IDE and Operating Substrate Vision

## Purpose

This document explains the product-level destination of ION:
- what it means inside the IDE today,
- what a mature IDE-native intelligence environment should feel like,
- and how that points toward a broader operating substrate.

## Current practical posture

Today, the canonical root is operated primarily through:
- an IDE-native working executor,
- `python -m kernel ...` operator surfaces,
- bounded packet and handoff artifacts,
- supervised runtime surfaces where appropriate,
- and low-burn sequential execution as the default carrier posture.

This is important.
ION is already an operating environment in miniature, not just a backlog for one.

## IDE-native intelligence

The mature IDE experience is not just chat plus file edits.

In the intended ION form, the IDE becomes a lawful continuity environment where:
- tasks are packetized,
- continuation is preserved across sessions,
- the next lawful step is explicit,
- future work remains structured,
- executors can take over lawfully,
- and the operator can supervise the orchestration field instead of micromanaging raw prompt state.

## Operator control as product behavior

The operator should be able to:
- inspect current posture,
- see next-step pressure,
- start or hold runtime carriage,
- review receipts,
- request packets,
- and understand what the system believes should happen next.

That is product behavior, not just internal architecture.

## Current carrier reality

The current root deliberately treats parallel subagent spawning as optional rather than baseline.

That means:
- low-burn sequential carriage is the default,
- manual fallback remains first-class,
- and wider multi-executor operation must earn legitimacy through stronger law and proof.

This prevents the product vision from outrunning continuity proof.

## IDE to substrate

The broader destination is larger than the IDE.

The long-term substrate should support:
- continuity across contexts,
- future-aware orchestration,
- lawful carrier switching,
- durable handoff,
- structured agent coordination,
- and eventually a system-wide intelligence fabric rather than isolated tool integrations.

## Why this matters

Most AI tooling is tool-first and intelligence-second.

ION is trying to invert that relationship:
- intelligence becomes a governed runtime substrate,
- and tools become surfaces attached to that substrate.

## What should not be faked

The operating-substrate vision does not justify:
- pretending swarm behavior is already principled,
- bypassing packet law,
- widening beyond proved continuation,
- or treating optional chassis features as canonical process.

Product vision must remain subordinate to continuity law.

## Near-term implication

The correct path from here is:
- strengthen takeover normalization on top of the now-explicit executor capability law,
- strengthen continuation proof,
- strengthen carrier equivalence proof,
- and only then widen into more ambitious orchestration behavior.

That is how the product destination becomes credible rather than merely ambitious.
