# ION System Overview

## Purpose

This document explains ION at the highest useful level.

It answers:
- what ION is,
- what problem it solves,
- what its major organs are,
- what is real today,
- and how to keep the rest of the repository mentally centered.

## Short thesis

ION is a continuity-governed AI operating substrate.

It does not treat intelligence as isolated prompt-response.
It treats intelligence as lawful continuity carried through bounded steps, explicit handoff, traceable state, and future-aware orchestration.

## The problem ION is solving

Most current AI systems fail in one or more of these ways:
- context is hidden and fragile,
- future work is carried as vague intention rather than structured state,
- execution changes law when the carrier changes,
- handoff depends on oral tradition,
- and traceability is weaker than the operational claim.

ION exists to replace that with a lawful loop in which:
- context is compiled explicitly,
- the next bounded step is chosen under law,
- continuity is externalized in packets and receipts,
- future pressure is retained in horizon and scheduling structures,
- and a fresh capable executor can continue without hidden reconstruction.

## The irreducible loop

At the center, ION is simple:
1. read lawful state,
2. compile bounded context,
3. determine the next lawful step,
4. execute one bounded step through an allowed carrier,
5. land, hold, or escalate the result,
6. preserve continuity artifacts and future structure,
7. continue.

That loop is invariant even as the embodiment grows.

## Main system organs

### Kernel

The kernel is the lawful substrate.
It owns continuity, authority, packet law, horizon law, enactment law, review and threshold law, recovery and replay law, and carrier constraints.

### Packet system

Packets are bounded continuity carriers.
They let a fresh executor know what happened, what remains, what to read, and what to do next.

### Horizon system

The horizon system holds future work pressure across immediate, near, and far windows without pretending all future work is already execution-ready.

### Scheduler

The scheduler is a kernel subsystem that progressively compiles future work under law.
After L0 and L1, this now includes explicit schedule posture plus first-pass executor capability binding.

### Runtime carriers

The same workflow may be carried by:
- the current working executor,
- the supervised runtime,
- an external bridge,
- or later other bounded executors.

Carrier changes must not change workflow law.

### Operator surface

The operator CLI and IDE context are how the organism becomes usable and governable in practice.

## What makes ION different

ION is not:
- just a planner,
- just a daemon,
- just a memory system,
- just a scheduler,
- or just an app wrapped around a model.

It is the lawfully governed loop beneath those embodiments.

The relevant center is not any single module family.
The center is continuity preserved under bounded process.
