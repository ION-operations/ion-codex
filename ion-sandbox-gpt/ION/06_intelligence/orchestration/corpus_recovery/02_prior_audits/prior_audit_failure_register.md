# Prior audit failure register

## Main repeating failure modes

### 1. Local coherence mistaken for global recovery
A branch or bundle became internally cleaner, but the total organism remained fractured across multiple roots.

### 2. False novelty
AI repeatedly rebuilt surfaces that already existed in older lines because the corpus was not searched first.

### 3. Silent supersession
Older preserved centers were effectively downgraded without explicit predecessor-loss accounting.

### 4. Incomplete core linkage recovery
The linkage between truename, domain, rank, protocol, template, context, transition, and receipt was not restored as one explicit center.

### 5. Current-branch normalization outran fractured-core reintegration
Work focused heavily on making the current branch coherent while leaving the runtime/API/session and manager/orchestrator/swarm centers outside that coherence.

### 6. Activation authority remained diffuse
No prior effort fully answered, in one place:
- what decides activation
- by what law
- from what state
- with what receipt

### 7. Values recovery was too weak or too late
The original Aether/ION smallest-law center and values precedence order were not restored early enough to govern later decisions.

## Why this matters
The project did not fail for lack of effort.
It failed to converge because the audits were not brutally explicit enough about:
- what was preserved where
- what was lost from predecessor lines
- which centers were only historical
- which current claims outran preserved reality
