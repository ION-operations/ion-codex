The historical center governs **template creation itself** as a permanent constitutional function.

create, update, restore, supersede, demote, and retire operations on existing or emerging template surfaces.

## Delta judgment

The restoration target is **not** a rollback to the historical wording alone.
The restoration target is a current-line meta-template center that regains the historical constitutional seriousness **while preserving** the current branch's mutation-control discipline.

That yields the following governing judgment:

1. `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_EVOLUTION_PROTOCOL.md` should no longer be treated as the whole explanation of current template governance.
2. `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_CHANGE.md` should remain the mutation carrier rather than the constitutional center.
3. A future current-line restoration should introduce a first-class meta-template constitution and a current-line `TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT` surface.
4. This pass does not create or ratify those active-law surfaces yet.

## Lawful restoration target

The current line likely needs **two distinct layers** instead of one overloaded bridge surface:

### Layer A — Meta-template constitution
A current-line surface that states why template development exists, what it governs, and how it relates to state-machine / compiler evolution.

### Layer B — Template mutation carrier
A current-line surface that continues to govern concrete template-surface changes with provenance and downstream accounting.

This split preserves the best of both centers.

## Non-lawful moves for this packet

Do **not** do any of the following in this pass:
- replace the current branch bridge surfaces with historical text wholesale,
- declare the historical center final canon without reintegration,
- mutate active template law surfaces,
- or collapse meta-template constitution and change-control carrier into one surface again.
