# Lane B — Activation-authority transition candidates

## Purpose

Name the smallest future current-line surfaces that would restore the historical executable activation center without discarding the current branch packet, capability, and handoff protections.

This is a candidate map only.
It is **not** an implementation pass.

## Candidate surface set

### 1. `ION/02_architecture/ACTIVATION_AUTHORITY_PROTOCOL.md`
Role:
- define activation authority as a first-class current-line function,
- clarify its relation to scheduler, allocator, operator entry, and handoff law,
- and state what activation can and cannot do under one-workflow doctrine.

### 2. `ION/02_architecture/EXECUTOR_LIFECYCLE_PROTOCOL.md`
Role:
- define lawful executor claim, entry, suspension, resume, release, and retirement semantics,
- restore lifecycle seriousness without importing old fleet theater wholesale,
- and bind lifecycle changes to receipts, review, and settlement.

### 3. Keep `ION/02_architecture/LAWFUL_ORCHESTRATION_SCHEDULER_PROTOCOL.md`
Role:
- preserve scheduler as future-work compilation rather than activation center,
- prevent silent drift where scheduling vocabulary absorbs execution authority.

### 4. Keep the M14-M16 chain
Role:
- preserve validated takeover entry, compact handoff capsule materialization, and fresh-executor rehearsal,
- keep continuation-entry law subordinate to any future activation-authority center rather than replaced by it.

### 5. Keep `ION/02_architecture/EXECUTOR_CAPABILITY_REGISTRY_PROTOCOL.md` and `ION/02_architecture/BOUNDED_MULTI_AGENT_ALLOCATOR_PROTOCOL.md`
Role:
- preserve explicit carrier binding, trust posture, and bounded fan-out controls,
- ensure any restored activation authority remains capability-aware and non-theatrical.

## Intended future relation

The future current line should likely read as:

1. `ACTIVATION_AUTHORITY_PROTOCOL.md` answers **what activation authority is** and what class of work it governs.
2. `EXECUTOR_LIFECYCLE_PROTOCOL.md` answers **how executors lawfully enter, pause, resume, and exit**.
3. `LAWFUL_ORCHESTRATION_SCHEDULER_PROTOCOL.md` answers **how future work is compiled and ordered**.
4. `EXECUTOR_CAPABILITY_REGISTRY_PROTOCOL.md` answers **who can carry which work**.
5. M14-M16 answer **how validated continuation becomes direct next-executor entry**.

## Why this split is safer

It prevents two opposite mistakes:
- mistaking current handoff richness for full activation restoration,
- and mistaking historical orchestrator/swarm vividness for sufficient lawful current architecture.

## Lawful next step after review

If this candidate map is accepted, the next bounded move should be a **surface-design packet** for `ACTIVATION_AUTHORITY_PROTOCOL.md` and `EXECUTOR_LIFECYCLE_PROTOCOL.md`, still landing in draft / proposal space before active-law adoption.
