# Lane A — Meta-template restoration transition candidates

## Purpose

Name the smallest future current-line surfaces that would restore the historical meta-template center without discarding the current branch bridge protections.

This is a candidate map only.
It is **not** an implementation pass.

## Candidate surface set

### 1. `ION/02_architecture/META_TEMPLATE_CONSTITUTION_PROTOCOL.md`
Role:
- define template development as a standing constitutional function,
- bind it to the broader workflow/kernel/state-machine picture,
- and clarify that template mutation carriers are downstream implementation-control surfaces, not the whole constitutional story.

### 2. `ION/07_templates/actions/TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT.md`
Role:
- restore a current-line executable template for creating new templates,
- preserve gap/relationship/failure/automation/registration structure,
- adapt wording to the current branch rather than copying history blindly.

### 3. Keep `ION/02_architecture/TEMPLATE_SURFACE_EVOLUTION_PROTOCOL.md`
Role:
- demote it from sole meta-template explanation to bridge/change-governance layer,
- preserve its change-control precision,
- use it as the law for how template surfaces mutate after a higher constitutional center exists.

### 4. Keep `ION/07_templates/actions/TEMPLATE_SURFACE_CHANGE.md`
Role:
- remain the concrete carrier for mutation events,
- continue to force provenance, target-surface naming, and downstream accounting.

## Intended future relation

The future current line should likely read as:

1. `META_TEMPLATE_CONSTITUTION_PROTOCOL.md` answers **why** template development exists and what class of work it governs.
2. `TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT.md` answers **how** a new template is lawfully conceived and registered.
3. `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_EVOLUTION_PROTOCOL.md` answers **how template surfaces change over time**.
4. `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_CHANGE.md` carries the concrete change packet for those mutations.

## Why this split is safer

It prevents two opposite mistakes:
- mistaking current bridge safety for full historical restoration,
- and mistaking historical constitutional depth for sufficient present-day change control.

## Lawful next step after review

If this candidate map is accepted, the next bounded move should be a **surface-design packet** for the two missing current-line restoration surfaces, still landing in draft / proposal space before active-law adoption.
