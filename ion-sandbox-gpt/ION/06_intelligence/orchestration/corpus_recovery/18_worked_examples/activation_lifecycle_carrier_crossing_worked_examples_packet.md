## Worked-example classes

### 1. Manual operator enactment
A bounded packet is requested directly by a human operator for immediate manual execution.

Review question:
Can the repo show that operator urgency does not bypass activation authority, and that lifecycle begins only after lawful crossing?

### 2. Daemon/service enactment
A daemon or supervised service shell receives a prepared task and appears operationally ready to run.

Review question:
Can the repo show that service-shell readiness is not activation authority, and that lifecycle claim/readiness/entry still remain downstream of activation?

### 3. External/API enactment
An external transport or API surface is available and the task is transport-compatible.

Review question:
Can the repo show that transport availability is not enactment permission, and that carrier changes invocation form but not the activation/lifecycle contract?

### 4. Continuation across carrier change
A previously lawful enactment path is returned or suspended in one carrier and later resumed or re-entered in another.

Review question:
Can the repo show when preserved authority plus validated continuity is sufficient, and when fresh activation is required?
