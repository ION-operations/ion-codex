## Judgment
The current branch clearly carries the heaviest **operator burden** and the broadest explicit governance/state surface.

The production precursor `ION` and `IONv2` both preserve much stronger **compaction disciplines**:
- fewer total files
- fewer active governance/index surfaces
- smaller startup-facing descriptive burden
- still-strong executable posture

This does **not** prove that the current branch is wrong.
It does prove that the current branch has traded compactness and obvious center visibility for:
- stronger explicit governance repair
- stronger current-phase startup law
- stronger self-description and recovery instrumentation

## Recovery implication
A future coherent center will likely need to preserve:
- the current branch's governance-explicit repairs
- the precursor pair's compact extracted-core discipline
- `IONv2`'s compact executable implementation discipline

If the project keeps only one of those three, it will continue to lose either:
- center visibility,
- constitutional compactness,
- or implementation compression.
