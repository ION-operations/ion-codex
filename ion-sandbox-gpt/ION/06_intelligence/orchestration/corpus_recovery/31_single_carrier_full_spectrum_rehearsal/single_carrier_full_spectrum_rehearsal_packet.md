# Single-carrier full-spectrum ION rehearsal packet

## Purpose

Use the landed Lane A bridge pair to evaluate one concrete scenario:

**A single capable LLM carries the whole ION experience by traversing
protocol-bound roles and persona/delivery modes sequentially in one chat.**

This packet tests whether the current bridge pair can classify that need without
inventing a new agent ontology or assuming parallelism is the default ideal.

## Scenario

The Sovereign clarified the target architecture:

- ION may eventually use many parallel workers, but all results still have to be
  sequenced, read, settled, and continued by a coherent system.
- The bigger target is that one capable carrier can do the entire system's work
  by following ION protocol.
- "Agents" are not primarily separate personalities; they are context/workflow
  modes carried by the system.
- In manual chat, the current LLM becomes Steward, Relay, Vizier, Vice, Nemesis,
  Mason, Thoth, Vestige, Scribe, or a persona voice only when it loads and acts
  under that role's law.

## Gap analysis under `META_TEMPLATE_CONSTITUTION_PROTOCOL.md`

### Action being performed

One carrier is being asked to present the full ION experience in chat:

- route and orchestrate as Steward,
- preserve intent as Relay,
- analyze architecture as Vizier,
- apply contradiction pressure as Vice,
- audit as Nemesis,
- implement as Mason,
- research as Thoth,
- excavate stale lineage as Vestige,
- perform mechanical hygiene as Scribe,
- and optionally deliver in a selected EUNOIA/persona voice.

### Current state

Current law permits pieces of this, but not the complete chat-facing pattern:

- sequential multi-role mount exists,
- Relay EUNOIA exists,
- current roster exists,
- historical persona templates exist,
- but no current-line full-spectrum chat mux surface says how a single carrier
  should traverse roles and delivery personas without corrupting authority.

### Frequency

High.

This is not rare edge behavior. It is the expected manual/default ION operating
mode whenever one strong LLM is doing the work.

### Impact if ungoverned

Without a governed surface, the system risks:

- mistaking persona voice for role authority,
- treating role labels as roleplay instead of bounded work modes,
- flattening Relay, Steward, and persona into one vague "assistant" voice,
- spawning unnecessary parallel agents when a sequential traversal is enough,
- or claiming multi-agent operation happened when one carrier actually did a
  sequential pass.

## Boundary judgment

This scenario does **not** require a new roster entity.

It requires a current-line surface for **single-carrier role/persona traversal**:

- roles are governed work contexts,
- personas are delivery/relationship profiles,
- the carrier can traverse multiple roles sequentially,
- parallel agents remain optional bounded carriers, not the definition of ION.
