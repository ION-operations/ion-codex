## Governing split

The current line likely needs **two** future protocol surfaces:

1. `META_TEMPLATE_CONSTITUTION_PROTOCOL.md`
2. `TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT.md`

The split matters because each surface answers a different architectural question.

### 1. Meta-template constitution
This is the constitutional surface for **why** template development exists.
It should govern:
- the standing role of template development in the organism,
- relation to workflow law, kernel law, and template-binding law,
- constitutional obligations around gap analysis, relationship mapping, failure anticipation, and registration,
- relation to compiler/state-machine seriousness,
- and the boundary between constitutional development and downstream mutation carriers.

It should **not** become a concrete change packet, a catch-all registry dump, or a vague philosophy note detached from executable obligations.

### 2. Template development
This is the operative surface for **how** a new template is lawfully developed.
It should govern:
- development intents and trigger classes,
- gap and relationship analysis,
- failure-mode and automation-hook design,
- registration and propagation obligations,
- evidence requirements before a new template surface becomes live,
- and relation to later template-surface mutation.

It should **not** replace template-surface change control, final ratification law, or generic task planning.

## Draft design judgment

The safest current-line restoration path is:

- introduce `META_TEMPLATE_CONSTITUTION_PROTOCOL.md` as the restored constitutional center,
- introduce `TEMPLATE_DEVELOPMENT.md` as the restored operative development surface,
- keep `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_EVOLUTION_PROTOCOL.md` as bridge/change-governance law,
- and keep `TEMPLATE_SURFACE_CHANGE.md` as the concrete mutation carrier.

That produces a four-layer template-governance stack:

1. one-workflow doctrine and template-binding truth
2. meta-template constitution
3. template development and registration
4. template-surface evolution and concrete change packets
