# Codex Official Agent and Support Topology

## Why this exists

The build has already been behaving as though Codex were the practical construction lead.
The missing piece was not capability. The missing piece was an explicit protocol saying
that the behavior is lawful, current, and bounded.

Without that protocol, the repo was vulnerable to a new kind of drift:

- Codex doing lead work while still being described as merely provisional,
- support roles being useful in practice but still half-described through older
  premium-parallel assumptions,
- and current operational truth living in memories and spend pressure rather than
  in official files.

## Findings

1. The active root already depends on Codex as the main high-capability router and
   builder. Formalization is catching up to reality, not inventing a new hierarchy.
2. The safest phrasing is not that Codex replaces the field. It is that Codex is the
   **official current-phase construction lead** while the wider field remains available
   as governed support and independent review.
3. Support-agent value splits cleanly into three classes:
   governance-depth, volume/implementation, and research/terrain. Making that split
   explicit prevents empty theater and keeps spend aligned with real value.
4. Mason and Thoth were still partly leaning on missing or off-root template references.
   Local template restoration is part of agent formalization, not separate cleanup.
5. The broader parallel documents should remain as future or selective-expansion models,
   but they need an explicit pointer to the Codex-led current protocol so fresh sessions
   stop overreading the target field as the active one.

## Implications

- Codex can now be treated as an official role for construction leadership without
  silently claiming ratification or independent audit.
- Support agents can be used more aggressively where they are cheap and valuable,
  because the law around that use is clearer.
- Future extraction into scheduler/dispatch services becomes easier because the current
  lead/support pattern is now named and bounded.

## Recommended next moves

1. Use the new protocol whenever deciding whether to keep work local in Codex or route
   it to Mason, Thoth, Atlas, Relay, Vestige, Vice, Nemesis, or Vizier.
2. Keep building the kernel runtime helpers under Codex lead, but start issuing more
   bounded support packets for audit, terrain sweeps, and mechanical implementation.
3. Restore any further missing local templates only when a real support role needs them,
   not by speculative catalog rebuilding.
