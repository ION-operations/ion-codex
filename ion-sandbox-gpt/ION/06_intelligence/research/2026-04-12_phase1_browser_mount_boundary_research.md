# Browser Mount Boundary Research

## Why this exists

The first Phase 1 disagreement drill needs one evidence-backed research pass on whether
browser ChatGPT should be mounted directly into a live branch role or remain external
and unmounted.

## Sources or surfaces considered

- `ION/02_architecture/ROLE_CHASSIS_MOUNT_PROTOCOL.md`
- `ION/02_architecture/EXTERNAL_EXECUTION_MCP_BRIDGE_PROTOCOL.md`
- `ION/02_architecture/EXTERNAL_ZIP_RETURN_BRIDGE_PROTOCOL.md`
- `ION/AGENT_CONTRACT.md`
- `ION/05_context/comms/kernel_router_runs/2026-04-12_phase1_mount_proof_and_context_feed/05_browser_external_unmounted_role_chassis_mount.md`
- `ION/06_intelligence/research/2026-04-12_phase1_template_governance_gate_surface_map.md`

## Findings

1. The active current-phase mount law explicitly states that browser ChatGPT remains
   `EXTERNAL_UNMOUNTED` by default until a lawful mount packet plus stronger evidence
   says otherwise.
2. The live branch already has lawful participation surfaces for browser-style work
   through the generic external execution bridge and the current-phase browser zip-return
   bridge.
3. The mount proof bundle already materialized a real `ROLE_CHASSIS_MOUNT` packet for
   browser ChatGPT in `EXTERNAL_UNMOUNTED` posture.
4. No current active spine surface names a settled browser boot, named browser binding,
   or stronger role law that would override the external-unmounted default.

## Implications

- the current dispute is not between two equally strong active laws
- the stronger current-phase evidence favors keeping browser ChatGPT external and
  unmounted
- the next useful proof is not another mount argument; it is one real external-return
  drill proving the bounded return path

## Recommended next moves

- reconcile the disagreement in favor of `EXTERNAL_UNMOUNTED` current-phase posture
- route browser participation through `EXTERNAL_RETURN` plus `PATCH_PACKAGE`
- revisit direct-role mount only if stronger branch authority later exists
