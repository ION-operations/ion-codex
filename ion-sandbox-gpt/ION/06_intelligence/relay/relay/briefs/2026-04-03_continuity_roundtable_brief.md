## How to frame the continuity crisis without distortion

**Say this:**

- The system is suffering from **overlapping continuity theories** and **surfaces doing too many jobs at once**, while **language and plans** often assume automation that **is not yet bound to the active root**.

**Do not say this (without qualification):**

- “We just need more agents / clones.” Scaling multiplies interpretation error when the memory contract is unsettled.
- “The root MINI/CAPSULE/STATUS are obviously the one true continuity store for every role.” That is **exactly what is under dispute**; Vizier now classifies root trio as **Vizier-curated projection / operator convenience**, with **`ION/agents/{role}/`** as **source**—while Nemesis’s **Manual Continuity Recovery Mode** still names the **root trio** as **authoritative for the consolidation program** until explicitly superseded. Those two framings are **compatible only if you name which law is temporary and who may write which surface.**

**Preserve the distinction:**

- **Source continuity** (per-agent lanes)
- **Projection** (compiled or curated shared views)
- **Archive / witness** (history, ION-BUILD, SOS-OPUS lineage)
- **Emergency manual substitute** (what the flat shared root has been functioning as when private lanes were missing)

The historical capsule inventory supports the older pattern: **private per-agent MINI/CAPSULE + compiled projections + rich history**—so “shared root only” was never the full ION story.
