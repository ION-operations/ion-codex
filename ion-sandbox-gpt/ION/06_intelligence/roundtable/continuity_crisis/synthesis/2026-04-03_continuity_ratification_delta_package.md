## 2. Law core (unchanged in substance, refined in wording)

The law core remains the same, with one important Clause 7 refinement:

### 1. Source continuity is private.
Each active role owns its own source continuity. No role reads or writes another role's source continuity.

### 2. Shared root continuity surfaces are projections or temporary manual substitutes.
Root-level continuity surfaces may be useful for operator visibility and coordination, but they are not the primary continuity body of every role.

### 3. Interchange happens through public channels only.
Roles coordinate through inbox tasks, signals, public intelligence artifacts, and explicit handoff/review packets. They do not communicate by direct mutation of another role's continuity.

### 4. Manual mode is real ION.
If automation is absent, continuity is maintained manually to protocol. Manual continuity is not outside the system. It is the system operating by hand.

### 5. Automation is shadow until proven.
No continuity automation becomes authoritative until it has been compared against lawful manual operation through at least one complete clean work cycle.

### 6. The chat-death test is mandatory.
If a role cannot begin a fresh chat, read its lawful continuity, and resume correctly, the continuity architecture is not yet functioning.

### 7. Continuity-sensitive release must not bypass the required governance chain.
For covered continuity-sensitive artifacts, release or ratification must not bypass the required governance chain: **Primary → Daimon → Nemesis**, with **Sovereign adjudication or ratification where the artifact class or unresolved dissent requires it.**

### Recovery-period clarification
During Manual Continuity Recovery Mode, the root continuity trio may function as temporary operator-authoritative projections for system visibility and coordination, but they are not the private source continuity of any role.
