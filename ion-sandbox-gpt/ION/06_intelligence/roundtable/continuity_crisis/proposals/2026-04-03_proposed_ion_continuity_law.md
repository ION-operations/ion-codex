# Proposed ION Continuity Law

> This is a roundtable proposal for ratification.
> It is intentionally short.
> It defines the continuity floor, not the entire protocol web.

## 1. Source continuity is private

Each active role owns its own source continuity.
No role reads or writes another role's source continuity.

## 2. Shared root continuity surfaces are projections or temporary manual substitutes

Root-level `ION/MINI.md`, `ION/CAPSULE.md`, and `ION/STATUS.md` may be useful for
operator visibility and coordination, but they are not the primary continuity body of every role.

## 3. Interchange happens through public channels only

Roles coordinate through lawful public channels such as inbox tasks, signals,
public intelligence artifacts, and explicit handoff or review packets.
They do not communicate by direct mutation of another role's continuity.

## 4. Manual mode is real ION

If automation is absent, continuity is maintained manually to protocol.
Manual continuity is not outside the system. It is the system operating by hand.

## 5. Automation is shadow until proven

No continuity automation becomes authoritative until it has been compared against
lawful manual operation through at least one complete clean work cycle.

## 6. The chat-death test is mandatory

If a role cannot begin a fresh chat, read its lawful continuity, and resume correctly,
the continuity architecture is not yet functioning.

## 7. Continuity-sensitive release must not bypass the required governance chain

For covered continuity-sensitive artifacts, release or ratification must not bypass
the required governance chain: Primary → Daimon → Nemesis, with Sovereign adjudication
or ratification where the artifact class or unresolved dissent requires it.

## Recovery-period clarification

During Manual Continuity Recovery Mode, the root continuity trio may function as
temporary operator-authoritative projections for system visibility and coordination.
This does **not** make them any role's private source continuity.

They remain:

- recovery-era operator surfaces
- not private memory bodies
- and not proof that unified runtime compilation is already active

## Scope of this law

This law does not itself settle:

- supervisor path-shape classification
- full template restoration
- proof of broader runtime readiness
- or expansion posture

Those remain governed by the companion recovery-conditions note.
