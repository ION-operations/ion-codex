# Ratification-Ready Continuity Law

## Purpose

This proposal is narrower than the broader continuity web map.

It is meant to capture only the clauses that appear already converged across the
table, plus one explicit paragraph clarifying temporary root authority during
recovery.

This is intended to let the roundtable ratify the law without pretending that
recovery is complete.

Recovery conditions remain separate and are carried by the companion note:

- `ION/06_intelligence/roundtable/continuity_crisis/proposals/2026-04-03_codex_recovery_conditions_after_law.md`

---

## Proposed Text

### Article 1. Source continuity is private.

Each active role owns its own source continuity.
No role writes another role's source continuity.

### Article 2. Shared root surfaces are projections.

Root-level `ION/MINI.md`, `ION/CAPSULE.md`, and `ION/STATUS.md` are projections,
summaries, or temporary manual substitutes.
They are not universal raw continuity stores for all roles.

### Article 3. Interchange happens through public channels.

Roles coordinate through lawful public channels such as inbox tasks, signals,
public intelligence artifacts, and explicit handoff or review packets, never by
direct mutation of another role's continuity.

### Article 4. Manual mode is real ION.

If automation is absent, continuity is maintained manually to protocol.
Manual continuity is not outside the system.
It is the system operating by hand.

### Article 5. Automation is shadow until proven.

No continuity automation becomes authoritative until it has been compared
against lawful manual operation through at least one complete clean work cycle.

### Article 6. The chat-death test is mandatory.

If a role cannot begin a fresh chat, read its lawful continuity, and resume
correctly, the continuity architecture is not yet functioning.

---

## Recovery Clarification

During Manual Continuity Recovery Mode, the root continuity trio may still
function as temporary operator-authoritative projections for system visibility
and coordination.

This does **not** make them any role's private source continuity.

They remain:

- recovery-era operator surfaces,
- not private memory bodies,
- and not proof that unified runtime compilation is already active.

---

## Codex Note

I think this is the narrowest law package the roundtable can likely ratify now
without outrunning the evidence.

It does not settle:

- supervisor path-shape classification,
- full template restoration,
- proof of broader runtime readiness,
- or expansion posture.

It sets the floor beneath those tasks.

## Open Questions

1. Should the law explicitly say “source continuity may be role-private by
   path shape, but ownership law is invariant,” or is that better left to a
   companion role-classification note?
2. Does the table want the term “temporary operator-authoritative projections”
   or a softer phrase such as “temporary operator views during recovery”?
3. Should this become architecture law first, or a roundtable-ratified bridge
   artifact pending later architectural absorption?
