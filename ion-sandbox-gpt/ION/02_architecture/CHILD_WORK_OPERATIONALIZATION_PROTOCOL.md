# CHILD WORK OPERATIONALIZATION PROTOCOL

## Purpose

Operationalize child-work issuance as a first-class **supervised automation** path.

This protocol governs the seam between:
- planner-manifest readiness,
- explicit child-spec intent,
- automation policy,
- operator control,
- supervised daemon service,
- durable service receipts.

It does **not** authorize an unattended multi-agent field.

## Core law

1. Child issuance remains subordinate to kernel truth.
2. Child issuance remains subordinate to route-state and automation-state law.
3. Child issuance may occur only through explicit child specs already bound to an accepted planner delta.
4. Policy and operator control must be evaluated before issuance.
5. Review pressure and spawn policy may require explicit approval.
6. Every supervised issuance attempt must produce a durable service receipt.

## Allowed supervised issuance modes

### 1. Manifest mode

A READY planner manifest may be selected explicitly and issued through supervised service.

### 2. Question/delta mode

A resolved question, committed parent work unit, and accepted child-bearing planner delta may be selected explicitly.
The service may compile the planner manifest and then issue through the same lawful path.

## Required checks

Before issuance, the service must evaluate:
- operator service mode,
- scope holds,
- automation policy for `ISSUE_CHILD_WORK`,
- runtime dispatch posture,
- runtime review pressure,
- spawn policy approval requirements.

## Required outputs

A lawful child-work service path must emit:
- durable receipt JSON,
- ledger row,
- policy evaluation,
- control-state snapshot,
- selected manifest/question/work-unit/delta identity,
- issued child work-unit and context-package ids when issuance succeeds.

## Non-goals

- no unattended swarm claim
- no hidden auto-issuance loop
- no external execution / MCP bridge yet
- no promotion of service receipts into kernel truth
